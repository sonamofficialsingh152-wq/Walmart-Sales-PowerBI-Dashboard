Executive summary

The dataset includes 6,435 store-week records across 45 stores, with total sales of $6.74B. Sales are concentrated in a relatively small number of stores, and the strongest explanatory variables are Store, CPI, unemployment, temperature, and fuel price.

Holiday weeks account for about 7.0% of observations, so the main business pattern is driven by normal trading periods rather than holiday spikes.

Key findings

1. Store concentration

A handful of stores generate a large share of sales, so ranking and comparing stores is essential for management action.

2. Time trends

Weekly trend analysis is the best way to show how sales evolve over the year and where holiday effects occur.

3. Predictive signal

The model explains a large portion of weekly sales variation, which makes it suitable for planning and prioritization.
